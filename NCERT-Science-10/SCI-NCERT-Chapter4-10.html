NCERT Solutions for Class 10 Science Chapter 4: Carbon and its Compounds (English Medium)

Page 61 In-Text Questions

Question 1

What would be the electron dot structure of carbon dioxide which has the formula CO₂?
Solution:
O = C = O (Double bonds between C and each O, each O with two lone pairs, C with no lone pairs).

Question 2

What would be the electron dot structure of a molecule of sulphur which is made up of eight atoms of sulphur? (Hint: The eight atoms of sulphur are joined together in the form of a ring).
Solution:
Eight S atoms in a ring, each with single bonds to adjacent S, and two lone pairs on each S.

Page 68 In-Text Questions

Question 1

How many structural isomers can you draw for pentane?
Solution:
Three structural isomers: n-pentane (straight chain), 2-methylbutane (isopentane), 2,2-dimethylpropane (neopentane).

Question 2

What are the two properties of carbon which lead to the huge number of carbon compounds we see around us?
Solution:
(i) Catenation: Ability to form long chains. (ii) Tetravalency: Four valence electrons, forms four bonds.

Question 3

What will be the formula and electron dot structure of cyclopentane?
Solution:
Formula: C₅H₁₀. Electron dot structure: Five C atoms in a ring, each bonded to two others and two H's, with appropriate lone pairs if needed, but all single bonds.

Page 71 In-Text Questions

Question 1

How is the conversion of ethanol to ethanoic acid an oxidation reaction?
Solution:
CH₃CH₂OH + [O] → CH₃CHO + H₂O (loss of H, oxidation); CH₃CHO + [O] → CH₃COOH (gain of O, oxidation).

Question 2

A mixture of oxygen and ethyne is burnt for welding. Can you tell why a mixture of ethyne and air is not used?
Solution:
Air has less oxygen (21%), leading to incomplete combustion and sooty flame; pure O₂ gives complete combustion and higher temperature (3000°C).

Page 74 In-Text Questions

Question 1

How would you distinguish experimentally between an alcohol and a carboxylic acid?
Solution:
Add NaHCO₃: Carboxylic acid gives CO₂ (effervescence), alcohol does not.

Question 2

What are oxidising agents?
Solution:
Substances that add oxygen or remove hydrogen from another substance (e.g., alkaline KMnO₄, acidified K₂Cr₂O₇).

Page 76 In-Text Questions

Question 1

Would you be able to check if water is hard by using a detergent?
Solution:
No, detergents do not form scum with Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺ ions in hard water.

Question 2

People use a variety of methods to wash clothes. Usually, after adding the soap, they ‘beat’ the clothes on a stone, or beat it with a paddle, scrub with a brush or the mixture is agitated in a washing machine. Why is agitation necessary to get clean clothes?
Solution:
Agitation helps in removing the dirt particles trapped in soap micelles by dislodging them from the fabric.

Question 1

Ethane, with the molecular formula C₂H₆, has
(a) 6 covalent bonds
(b) 7 covalent bonds

Butanone is a four-carbon compound with the functional group
(a) carboxylic acid
(b) aldehyde
(c) ketone
(d) alcohol
Solution:
(c) ketone

Question 3

While cooking, if the bottom of the vessel is getting blackened on the outside, it means that
(a) the food is not cooked completely
(b) the fuel is not burning completely
(c) the fuel is wet
(d) the fuel is burning completely
Solution:
(b) the fuel is not burning completely

Question 4

Explain the nature of the covalent bond using the bond formation in CH₃Cl.
Solution:
Covalent bond: Sharing of electrons. In CH₃Cl, C shares one electron with each of three H and one with Cl to complete octet.

Question 5

Draw the electron dot structures for
(a) ethanoic acid
(b) H₂S
(c) propanone
(d) F₂
Solution:
(a) CH₃COOH: C-C(=O)-OH with dots.
(b) H-S-H (two lone pairs on S).
(c) CH₃-C(=O)-CH₃.
(d) F:F (three lone pairs each).

Question 6

What is a homologous series? Explain with an example.
Solution:
Series of compounds with same functional group, similar properties, differ by -CH₂- unit. Example: Alkanes - CH₄, C₂H₆, C₃H₈, etc.

Question 8

Why does micelle formation take place when soap is added to water? Will a micelle be formed in other solvents such as ethanol also?
Solution:
Hydrophilic head dissolves in water, hydrophobic tail avoids it, forming spherical micelles. No in ethanol, as soap is soluble without micelle formation.

Question 9

Why are carbon and its compounds used as fuels for most applications?
Solution:
High calorific value, easy combustion, produce CO₂ and H₂O.

Question 10

Explain the formation of scum when hard water is treated with soap.
Solution:
Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺ + 2RCOO⁻ Na⁺ → (RCOO⁻)₂Ca/Mg (insoluble scum) + 2Na⁺.

Question 11

What change will you observe if you test soap with litmus paper (red and blue)?
Solution:
Basic solution: Red litmus turns blue; blue remains blue.

Question 12

What is hydrogenation? What is its industrial application?
Solution:
Addition of H₂ to unsaturated hydrocarbons (e.g., vegetable oils to vanaspati ghee). Application: Food industry.

Question 13

Which of the following hydrocarbons undergo addition reactions: C₂H₆, C₃H₈, C₃H₆, C₂H₂ and CH₄?
Solution:
C₃H₆ (propene), C₂H₂ (ethyne) - unsaturated.

Question 14

Give a test that can be used to differentiate between saturated and unsaturated hydrocarbons.
Solution:
Bromine water test: Unsaturated decolourise red-brown Br₂ water; saturated do not.

Question 15

Explain the mechanism of the cleaning action of soaps.
Solution:
Hydrophobic tails attach to dirt/grease, hydrophilic heads form micelle in water; agitation removes micelle with dirt.
