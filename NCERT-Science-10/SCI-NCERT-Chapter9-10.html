NCERT Solutions for Class 10 Science Chapter 9: Light – Reflection and Refraction

In-Text Questions

Question 1

Define the principal focus of a concave mirror.
Solution:
Principal focus is the point on the principal axis where rays parallel to principal axis converge after reflection.

Question 2

The radius of curvature of a spherical mirror is 20 cm. What is its focal length?
Solution:
F = R/2 = 20/2 = 10 cm.

Question 3

Name a mirror that can give an erect and enlarged image of an object.
Solution:
A concave mirror can give erect and enlarged image when object is placed between pole and focus.

Question 4

Why do we prefer a convex mirror as a rear-view mirror?
Solution:
Because convex mirrors give wider field of view and always give erect images.

Question 5

A ray of light travelling in air enters water. Will it bend towards or away from the normal?
Solution:
Light bends **towards the normal** when it enters denser medium (air → water).

Question 6

Refractive index of water is 1.33 and glass is 1.52. In which does light travel faster?
Solution:
Light travels faster in medium with lower refractive index → **Water**.

Question 7

Define 1 dioptre of power of lens.
Solution:
One dioptre = power of lens whose focal length is 1 metre.

Question 8

A convex lens forms a real and inverted image of a needle at 50 cm from the lens. Where should the needle be placed so that image is magnified and erect?
Solution:
To get erect and magnified image, object must be placed **between optical centre and focus (between O and F)**.

Exercise Questions

Question 1

Which one of the following materials cannot be used to make a lens?
(a) Glass (b) Plastic (c) Clay
Solution: (c) Clay

Question 2

The image formed by a concave mirror is observed to be virtual, erect and larger than the object. Where should the object be placed?
Solution:
Object should be placed **between pole (P) and focus (F)** of a concave mirror.

Question 3

Where should an object be placed in front of convex lens to get real, inverted and same-size image?
Solution:
Object should be placed at **2F**.

Question 4

A concave lens always forms a ___ image.
Solution: **Virtual and erect**

Question 5

Use the mirror formula to determine the image distance:
u = –10 cm, f = –30 cm (concave mirror)
Solution:
Mirror formula: 1/f = 1/v + 1/u 1/–30 = 1/v + 1/–10 1/v = –1/30 + 1/10 = 2/30 = 1/15 v = **15 cm** Image is real and inverted.

Question 6

An object 5 cm in length is placed at distance 20 cm in front of convex mirror of focal length 20 cm. Find image position and size.
Solution:
Given: u = –20 cm, f = +20 cm 1/v = 1/f – 1/u = 1/20 + 1/20 = 1/10 v = **10 cm** (behind mirror) Magnification m = v/u = 10/–20 = –1/2 Image height = m × h = –1/2 × 5 = **–2.5 cm** Image is virtual, erect and smaller.

Question 7

A dentist uses a mirror of focal length 5 cm to examine a tooth. What is the nature and position of image?
Solution:
Dentists use **concave mirror**. To get enlarged erect image → object must be between P and F. Image is **virtual, erect and enlarged** behind mirror.

Question 8

A convex lens of focal length 15 cm forms an image of a candle placed at 30 cm. Calculate image distance.
Solution:
u = –30 cm, f = 15 cm 1/v = 1/f – 1/u = 1/15 – (–1/30) 1/v = 1/15 + 1/30 = 3/30 = 1/10 v = **10 cm** Image is real and inverted.

Question 9

What is power of lens with focal length –2 m?
Solution:
P = 1/f (in metres) P = 1/–2 = –0.5 D (concave lens)
