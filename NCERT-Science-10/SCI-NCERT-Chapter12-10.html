NCERT Solutions for Class 10 Science Chapter 12: Magnetic Effects of Electric Current

In-Text Questions

Question 1

What is a magnetic field? How can its direction be represented?
Solution:
A magnetic field is a region around a magnet or a current-carrying conductor in which magnetic forces can be detected. The direction of a magnetic field is shown by **field lines**. By convention, the field lines emerge from the **north pole** and enter the **south pole**.

Question 2

What is the shape of magnetic field lines around a long straight current-carrying conductor?
Solution:
The magnetic field lines around a straight conductor are **concentric circles** with the conductor passing through the center.

Question 3

How can the direction of magnetic field be determined around a straight conductor?
Solution:
The **Right-Hand Thumb Rule** is used: - Hold the conductor with your right hand with the **thumb pointing in the direction of current**. - The curled fingers show the **direction of magnetic field** around the conductor.

Question 4

What is a solenoid? What is the shape of its magnetic field?
Solution:
A solenoid is a **coil of many circular turns of insulated wire** wound in the shape of a cylinder. The magnetic field inside a solenoid is **uniform and parallel**, while outside it is similar to a **bar magnet**.

Question 5

A compass needle is placed near a current-carrying conductor. What happens to it?
Solution:
The compass needle **deflects** due to the magnetic field produced by the current. The direction of deflection depends on the **current direction**.

Question 6

State the rule to find the direction of force on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field.
Solution:
The **Left-Hand Rule (Fleming’s Left-Hand Rule)**: - Stretch **forefinger** in the direction of magnetic field, - **Middle finger** in the direction of current, - **Thumb** shows the direction of **force** on the conductor.

Question 7

Define electromagnetic induction.
Solution:
Electromagnetic induction is the **process of producing current in a conductor** by changing the **magnetic field** around it.

Question 8

How can we induce current in a coil?
Solution:
Current can be induced in a coil by: 1. Moving a **magnet** towards or away from the coil. 2. Moving the **coil** in a **stationary magnetic field**. 3. Changing **current** in a nearby coil (mutual induction).

Question 9

What is the principle of an electric motor?
Solution:
An electric motor works on **Fleming’s Left-Hand Rule**: A current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field experiences a **mechanical force**, which produces rotation.

Question 10

What is the function of a split ring in a DC motor?
Solution:
The split ring (commutator) **reverses the direction of current** every half rotation to ensure the motor rotates **continuously in one direction**.

Question 11

State the principle of an electric generator.
Solution:
An electric generator works on **Faraday’s law of electromagnetic induction**: A changing magnetic field induces **electric current** in a coil.

Question 12

What is the function of brushes in a DC generator?
Solution:
Brushes **maintain electrical contact** with the rotating coil (via commutator) to transfer current to external circuit.

Exercise Questions

Question 1

State the rule to find the direction of magnetic field around a current-carrying conductor.
Solution:
The **Right-Hand Thumb Rule** is used: Thumb points in the **direction of current**, curled fingers show the **direction of magnetic field**.

Question 2

Draw magnetic field lines around a current-carrying straight conductor.
Solution:
The magnetic field lines are **concentric circles** around the conductor with direction given by Right-Hand Thumb Rule.

Question 3

Draw magnetic field lines around a circular current loop.
Solution:
Magnetic field lines **inside the loop** are parallel and concentrated; outside, they curve from one side of the loop to the other, resembling a **bar magnet**.

Question 4

Explain the construction and working of a solenoid.
Solution:
- A **solenoid** is a cylindrical coil of many turns. - When current flows, it produces a **magnetic field similar to a bar magnet**. - If a **soft iron core** is inserted, the field becomes stronger.

Question 5

Explain Fleming’s left-hand rule.
Solution:
- Stretch **forefinger** in magnetic field direction. - **Middle finger** in current direction. - **Thumb** points in force direction (motion of conductor).

Question 6

Explain electromagnetic induction.
Solution:
When a conductor experiences a **changing magnetic field**, **induced current** flows in it. Applications: AC generator, transformer.

Question 7

What is the difference between AC and DC?
Solution:
- **AC (Alternating Current):** direction of current changes periodically. - **DC (Direct Current):** current flows in a single direction.

Question 8

Explain the function of brushes in a DC motor and generator.
Solution:
Brushes maintain **electrical contact** with the rotating coil via the **commutator** and allow current to flow to external circuit.

Question 9

Explain the principle of an electric motor.
Solution:
A current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field experiences a **force**. This mechanical force produces **rotation** (motor works on this principle).

Question 10

Explain the principle of an electric generator.
Solution:
A **changing magnetic field** induces current in a coil (Faraday’s Law). Rotating a coil in a magnetic field produces electricity.

Question 11

Explain the role of split ring commutator in a DC motor.
Solution:
It **reverses current** in the coil after half rotation, ensuring **continuous rotation in one direction**.

Question 12

Why is a soft iron core used in a solenoid?
Solution:
The soft iron core **enhances the magnetic field strength** inside the solenoid.

Question 13

Name two applications of electromagnets.
Solution:
1. Electric bells 2. Lifting heavy iron scrap in junkyards

Question 14

What will happen if a current-carrying conductor is placed parallel to a magnetic field?
Solution:
If the conductor is **parallel**, the force is zero (F = BIL sinθ; θ = 0 → sin0 = 0).

Question 15

State Fleming’s right-hand rule.
Solution:
- Forefinger → Magnetic field - Thumb → Motion of conductor - Middle finger → Induced current

Question 16

What is the effect of increasing number of turns in a solenoid?
Solution:
The **magnetic field strength increases** proportionally with the number of turns.

Question 17

Explain how an electric bell works using electromagnet.
Solution:
- Pressing switch sends current through electromagnet. - Electromagnet attracts hammer → strikes bell. - Break in circuit resets hammer → repeats → ringing continues.

Question 18

Why is AC preferred over DC for long-distance transmission?
Solution:
AC voltage can be easily **stepped up or down** using transformers → less power loss → suitable for long distances.
