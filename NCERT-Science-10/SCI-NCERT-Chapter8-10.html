NCERT Solutions for Class 10 Science Chapter 8: Heredity

In-Text Questions

Question 1

If a trait A exists in 10% of a population and trait B exists in 60% of the same population, which trait is likely to have arisen earlier?
Solution:
Trait B is likely to have arisen earlier because traits that appear earlier have more time to spread in the population.

Question 2

How does the creation of variations in a species promote survival?
Solution:
Variations help species survive sudden environmental changes. Some individuals may possess favorable variations that help them survive and reproduce.

Question 3

How do Mendel’s experiments show that traits are inherited independently?
Solution:
Mendel’s dihybrid cross showed independent assortment: – Two traits were crossed – Offspring showed new combinations (9:3:3:1 ratio) This proved traits are inherited independently.

Question 4

A man with blood group A marries a woman with blood group O. They have a daughter with blood group O and a son with blood group A. What are the possible genotypes of the father?
Solution:
Blood group O child must get **‘i’ allele** from both parents. Mother = ii Daughter = ii So father must contribute **i**, meaning father is **Ai (heterozygous)**.

Question 5

How is the sex of the child determined in human beings?
Solution:
Male gametes carry either X or Y chromosomes. Female gametes carry only X. • X from male + X from female → girl (XX) • Y from male + X from female → boy (XY) Thus, sex of child is determined by father.

Exercise Questions

Question 1

If a trait is present in 30% of a population, can we conclude that this trait has no survival advantage?
Solution:
No. Prevalence alone does not determine survival advantage; many factors influence trait frequency.

Question 2

Why are traits acquired during lifetime not inherited?
Solution:
Acquired traits affect body cells, not reproductive cells. Only traits in DNA of germ cells are inherited.

Question 3

Why are the small numbers of surviving tigers a cause of worry from the point of view of genetics?
Solution:
Small populations have low genetic variation, making species vulnerable to diseases and environmental changes.

Question 4

What are the different patterns of inheritance observed in Mendel’s experiments?
Solution:
• Dominance • Segregation • Independent assortment These explain how traits pass from parents to offspring.

Question 5

How does Mendel’s experiment show that traits may be dominant or recessive?
Solution:
When pure tall and pure short plants were crossed, F₁ plants were all tall. Short trait reappeared in F₂. Thus tall = dominant, short = recessive.

Question 6

How do Mendel’s experiments show that traits are inherited independently?
Solution:
In dihybrid cross, new combinations appeared in 9:3:3:1 ratio → independent assortment.

Question 7

A man with blood group A marries a woman with blood group B. They have a child with blood group O. What are the possible genotypes of the parents?
Solution:
For child to be O (ii), both must give ‘i’. Father = Ai Mother = Bi Possible genotypes: **Ai and Bi**.

Question 8

How is sex determined in human beings?
Solution:
Male determines sex by contributing either X or Y chromosome during fertilization.
