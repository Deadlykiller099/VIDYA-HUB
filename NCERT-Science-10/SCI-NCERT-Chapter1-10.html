NCERT Solutions for Class 10 Science

Chapter 3: Metals and Non-metals (English Medium)

Page 40 In-Text Questions

Question 1

Explain the meaning of malleability and ductility. Why are they important in industrial applications?
Solution:
Malleability: Ability of metals to be beaten into thin sheets. Ductility: Ability of metals to be drawn into thin wires. These properties allow metals to be shaped into sheets and wires for industrial uses like utensils, wires, etc.

Question 2

Discuss the arrangement of elements in the Periodic Table based on their chemical properties.
Solution:
Metals are placed on the left side, non-metals on the right, and metalloids in between. Reactivity increases down the group for metals and up the group for non-metals.

Question 3

Why do ionic compounds have high melting points?
Solution:
Ionic compounds have strong electrostatic forces of attraction between ions, requiring high energy to break, hence high melting points.

Page 42 In-Text Questions

Question 1

How do metals and non-metals react with oxygen? Give examples.
Solution:
Metals react with oxygen to form basic oxides: 4Na + O₂ → 2Na₂O. Non-metals form acidic oxides: S + O₂ → SO₂.

Page 46 In-Text Questions

Question 1

What types of oxides are formed when non-metals and metals react with hydrogen?
Solution:
Non-metals form covalent hydrides (e.g., HCl). Metals form ionic hydrides (e.g., NaH).

Page 49 In-Text Questions

Question 1

Define an amphoteric oxide. Give examples.
Solution:
Amphoteric oxides react with both acids and bases. Examples: Al₂O₃, ZnO.

Question 2

What is the aqueous solution of aluminium oxide called? How does it react with acids and bases?
Solution:
Aluminium oxide is insoluble, but its hydroxide forms aluminate with base and salt with acid.

Page 51 In-Text Questions

Question 1

Name two metals that can displace hydrogen from dilute acids. Write equations.
Solution:
Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂
Fe + 2HCl → FeCl₂ + H₂

Exercises (Page 53-55)

Question 1

Which of the following statements is correct?
(i) All the properties of metals and non-metals can be explained in terms of their position in the Periodic Table.
(ii) Aluminium is a highly reactive metal, yet it is used to make utensils for cooking.
(iii) The oxide of sulphur is acidic in nature.
(iv) All metals have high melting points.
Solution:
(ii) and (iii)

Question 2

Which of the following is not a general property of metals?
(a) High density (b) High malleability (c) High ductility (d) Good conductor of heat
Solution:
(a) High density (Some metals like sodium have low density)

Question 3

Which of the following metals can displace hydrogen from water?
(a) Sodium (b) Magnesium (c) Aluminium (d) All of the above
Solution:
(d) All of the above

Question 4

Aluminium is used for making cooking utensils. Which of the following properties of aluminium are responsible for the same?
(i) Good thermal conductivity (ii) Good electrical conductivity (iii) Ductility (iv) High melting point
Solution:
(i) and (iii)

Question 5

The most abundant metal in the earth’s crust is
(a) Iron (b) Aluminium (c) Calcium (d) Sodium
Solution:
(b) Aluminium

Question 6

Which of the following non-metals is lustrous?
(a) Sulphur (b) Oxygen (c) Nitrogen (d) Iodine
Solution:
(d) Iodine

Question 7

Which of the following statements is not correct?
(a) All metals are solid at room temperature. (b) All non-metals are solid at room temperature. (c) All metals are good conductors of heat. (d) None of the above
Solution:
(b) All non-metals are solid at room temperature. (Some like bromine are liquid)

Question 8

Which of the following is not a characteristic property of metals?
(a) High density (b) High malleability (c) Low melting point (d) Good conductor of electricity
Solution:
(c) Low melting point (Most metals have high melting points)

Question 9

Which of the following statements is correct?
(a) Metals can be drawn into thin wires. (b) Metals are good conductors of heat and electricity. (c) Metals are sonorous. (d) All of the above
Solution:
(d) All of the above

Question 11

Why do ionic compounds have high melting and boiling points?
Solution:
Due to strong electrostatic forces between oppositely charged ions.

Question 12

Write the formulae of the following compounds and state the type of bonding:
(i) Calcium oxide (ii) Mercury (II) chloride (iii) Copper (II) sulphate (iv) Aluminium chloride
Solution:
(i) CaO (Ionic) (ii) HgCl₂ (Ionic) (iii) CuSO₄ (Ionic) (iv) AlCl₃ (Covalent)

Question 13

How does the reactivity series help in predicting chemical behaviour of metals?
Solution:
Metals higher in the series are more reactive and can displace lower ones from compounds.

Question 14

Why do metals form basic oxides?
Solution:
Metals lose electrons easily to form positive ions, leading to basic oxides.

Question 15

Name two metals that are found in nature in the free state. Why are they so found?
Solution:
Gold and silver. They are least reactive and do not react with air, water, etc.

Question 16

What is an alloy? Why are alloys commonly used in electrical devices?
Solution:
Alloy: Homogeneous mixture of metals. Used for better properties like higher resistance or strength.

Question 17

What is the corrosion of iron called? How can it be prevented?
Solution:
Rusting. Prevented by painting, galvanising, alloying.

Question 18

What is anodising? How does it protect the metal?
Solution:
Electrochemical process forming oxide layer on aluminium. Protects from corrosion.

Question 19

Distinguish between calcination and roasting. Give one example each.
Solution:
Calcination: Heating in absence of air to remove water/volatiles (e.g., 2Fe₂O₃.3H₂O → Fe₂O₃ + 3H₂O + heat).
Roasting: Heating in presence of air for sulphides to oxides (e.g., 2ZnS + 3O₂ → 2ZnO + 2SO₂).

Question 20

Why is carbon used as a reducing agent? Give example.
Solution:
Carbon is cheap and can reduce metal oxides to metals. Example: Fe₂O₃ + 3C → 2Fe + 3CO.
