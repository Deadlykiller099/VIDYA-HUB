NCERT Solutions for Class 10 Science Chapter 10: The Human Eye and the Colourful World

In-Text Questions

Question 1

What is meant by power of accommodation of the eye?
Solution:
Power of accommodation is the ability of the eye lens to adjust its focal length to focus near and distant objects clearly.

Question 2

A person with hypermetropia can see distant objects clearly but cannot see nearby objects distinctly. What kind of lenses are required to correct the defect?
Solution:
Hypermetropia is corrected using a **convex lens** (converging lens).

Question 3

What is the near point and far point of the human eye with normal vision?
Solution:
Near point = 25 cm
Far point = infinity

Question 4

A student reads the time on his watch as 8:30. He holds the watch in front of his eyes and again reads 8:30. What does this show about the power of accommodation?
Solution:
It shows the eye can adjust focal length to clearly see both near and distant objects due to power of accommodation.

Question 5

The far point of a myopic person is 80 cm. What power of lens is needed to correct the defect?
Solution:
For myopia: f = –0.8 m Power P = 1/f = –1.25 D A concave lens of **–1.25 D** is required.

Question 6

What causes the twinkling of stars?
Solution:
Twinkling is caused by atmospheric refraction—light bends irregularly due to varying air densities.

Question 7

Explain why the planets do not twinkle.
Solution:
Planets appear as extended sources (large angular size). Fluctuations of light rays cancel out → no twinkling.

Question 8

Why does the sky appear dark instead of blue to an astronaut?
Solution:
There is no atmosphere in space → no scattering of light → sky appears black.

Exercise Questions

Question 1

Define the power of a lens.
Solution:
Power of a lens is the reciprocal of its focal length (in metres). P = 1/f Unit: dioptre (D).

Question 2

A student cannot read a blackboard from 4 m but can read from 2 m. What defect does he have?
Solution:
He is myopic (near-sighted).

Question 3

What is the far point and near point of the human eye with normal vision?
Solution:
Near point = 25 cm Far point = infinity

Question 4

A person with myopia has far point 80 cm. What lens will correct this?
Solution:
Concave lens of power –1.25 D (as calculated earlier).

Question 5

What are the causes of presbyopia?
Solution:
Due to ageing: • weakening of ciliary muscles • loss of flexibility of eye lens

Question 6

Explain atmospheric refraction with two examples.
Solution:
Atmospheric refraction: bending of light due to varying densities in atmosphere.
Examples:
1. Twinkling of stars.
2. Early sunrise and delayed sunset.

Question 7

Explain why the sky appears blue.
Solution:
Shorter wavelengths (blue) scatter more in atmosphere → sky appears blue.

Question 8

Why does the sun appear reddish at sunrise and sunset?
Solution:
Light travels longer path → blue light scatters away → only red light reaches eyes.

Question 9

The far point of a student is 5 m. What is the nature and power of lens required?
Solution:
f = –5 m P = –0.2 D Concave lens of **–0.2 D**.
