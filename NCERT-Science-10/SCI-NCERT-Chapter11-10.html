NCERT Solutions for Class 10 Science Chapter 11: Electricity

In-Text Questions

Question 1

What does an electric current mean?
Solution:
Electric current is the rate of flow of electric charge through a conductor. I = Q / t.

Question 2

Define the unit of current.
Solution:
The SI unit of current is **ampere (A)**. 1 A = 1 coulomb of charge flowing per second.

Question 3

Calculate the number of electrons constituting one coulomb of charge.
Solution:
Charge of 1 electron = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C Number of electrons = 1 / 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ = 6.25 × 10¹⁸ electrons.

Question 4

Name a device that helps to maintain a potential difference across a conductor.
Solution:
A **cell**, **battery**, or **generator** maintains potential difference.

Question 5

What is the SI unit of potential difference?
Solution:
Volt (V).

Question 6

Define 1 volt.
Solution:
1 volt is the potential difference when 1 joule of work moves 1 coulomb of charge. 1 V = 1 J/C.

Question 7

How much energy is given to each coulomb of charge passing through a 6 V battery?
Solution:
Energy = V × Q = 6 × 1 = 6 J.

Question 8

On what factors does the resistance of a conductor depend?
Solution:
Resistance depends on: • Length of conductor • Area of cross-section • Type of material • Temperature

Question 9

Will current flow more easily through a thick wire or thin wire of same material and length?
Solution:
More easily through a **thick wire** because resistance is lower.

Question 10

Which has more resistance – a 10 m long copper wire or a 5 m long copper wire?
Solution:
The **10 m wire** has more resistance (R ∝ L).

Question 11

Draw V–I graph for an ohmic resistor.
Solution:
A straight line passing through origin, showing V ∝ I.

Question 12

How does resistance change in a series combination?
Solution:
Total resistance increases: R = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ …

Question 13

How does resistance change in a parallel combination?
Solution:
Total resistance decreases: 1/R = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + …

Exercise Questions

Question 1

A 10 Ω resistor carries 2 A current. Calculate voltage across it.
Solution:
V = IR = 2 × 10 = 20 V.

Question 2

What is the SI unit of electric power?
Solution:
Watt (W).

Question 3

An electric bulb draws 0.5 A at 220 V. What is its power?
Solution:
P = VI = 220 × 0.5 = 110 W.

Question 4

What resistance must be added to 4 Ω resistor to get 8 Ω total in series?
Solution:
R = 8 – 4 = 4 Ω.

Question 5

How much energy is consumed by a 100 W bulb in 5 hours?
Solution:
Energy = P × t = 100 × 5 = 500 Wh = 0.5 kWh.

Question 6

Calculate the power consumed when 3 A current flows through 220 V line.
Solution:
P = VI = 220 × 3 = 660 W.

Question 7

A heater has 1000 W, 220 V rating. Calculate resistance.
Solution:
P = V² / R → R = V² / P R = 220² / 1000 = 48.4 Ω.

Question 8

Explain series and parallel combinations with advantages.
Solution:
Series:
• Same current
• Voltages add Disadvantage: If one device fails, circuit breaks. Parallel:
• Same voltage
• Currents add Advantage: Devices work independently; used in homes.
