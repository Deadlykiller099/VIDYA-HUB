NCERT Solutions for Class 10 Science Chapter 7: How Do Organisms Reproduce?

In-Text Questions

Question 1

What is the importance of DNA copying in reproduction?
Solution: DNA copying ensures transfer of genetic information from parents to offspring and produces variations needed for evolution.

Question 2

Why is variation beneficial to the species but not necessarily for the individual?
Solution: Variations help species survive changing environments, but an individual may or may not benefit from a particular variation.

Question 3

How does binary fission differ from multiple fission?
Solution: In binary fission, one parent splits into two; in multiple fission, one parent divides into many daughter cells.

Question 4

How will an organism be benefited if it reproduces through spores?
Solution: Spores can survive harsh conditions, spread easily, and help organisms multiply quickly.

Question 5

Can you think of reasons why more complex organisms cannot give rise to new individuals through regeneration?
Solution: Complex organisms have organ systems and cannot reorganize their bodies from small fragments; regeneration is limited to repair.

Question 6

Why is vegetative propagation practiced for growing some plants?
Solution: It produces genetically identical plants, helps grow plants quickly, and preserves desirable traits.

Question 7

Why is DNA copying an essential part of the process of reproduction?
Solution: Without DNA copying, characteristics of the parent cannot pass to offspring.

Question 8

How is the process of pollination different from fertilization?
Solution: Pollination is transfer of pollen to stigma; fertilization is fusion of male and female gametes.

Question 9

What is the role of the seminal vesicles and prostate gland?
Solution: They add fluid to sperm to provide nourishment and aid in transport.

Question 10

What are the changes seen in girls at puberty?
Solution: Breast development, menstruation, widening of hips, and growth of hair in pubic and underarm regions.

Question 11

How does the embryo get nourishment inside the mother's body?
Solution: The placenta provides nutrients and oxygen from mother to embryo.

Question 12

If a woman is using a copper-T, will it help in protecting her from sexually transmitted diseases?
Solution: No. Copper-T prevents pregnancy but does not protect from STDs.

Exercise Questions

Question 1

Asexual reproduction takes place through budding in
(a) Amoeba (b) Yeast (c) Plasmodium (d) Leishmania
Solution: (b) Yeast

Question 2

Which of the following is not a part of the female reproductive system?
(a) Ovary (b) Uterus (c) Vas deferens (d) Fallopian tube
Solution: (c) Vas deferens

Question 3

The anther contains
(a) Sepals (b) Ovules (c) Pistil (d) Pollen grains
Solution: (d) Pollen grains

Question 4

What are the advantages of sexual reproduction over asexual reproduction?
Solution: Sexual reproduction introduces variation, increases adaptability, and promotes evolution.

Question 5

What are the functions of the testis in human beings?
Solution: Testes produce sperm and the hormone testosterone.

Question 6

Why does menstruation occur?
Solution: If fertilization does not occur, the uterine lining breaks down and is released as menstruation.

Question 7

Draw a labeled diagram of the human male reproductive system.
Solution: Draw the diagram with labels: Testis, Scrotum, Vas deferens, Seminal vesicle, Prostate gland, Urethra, Penis.

Question 8

What are the different methods of contraception?
Solution: Barrier methods, chemical methods, IUDs (copper-T), and surgical methods.

Question 9

How are the modes for reproduction different in unicellular and multicellular organisms?
Solution: Unicellular organisms use asexual reproduction (binary fission, budding). Multicellular organisms generally use sexual reproduction.

Question 10

How does reproduction help in providing stability to populations of species?
Solution: Reproduction replaces dying individuals and maintains population size in an ecosystem.

Question 11

What could be the reasons for adopting contraceptive methods?
Solution: Family planning, preventing unwanted pregnancies, controlling population, and preventing STDs.
