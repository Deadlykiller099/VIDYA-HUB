NCERT Solutions for Class 10 Science Chapter 6: Control and Coordination (English Medium)

In-Text Questions

Question 1

What is a reflex action? Give two examples.
Solution:
A reflex action is a quick, automatic, and involuntary response to a stimulus that does not involve conscious thinking.

Examples:
1. Withdrawing hand when touching something hot.
2. Knee-jerk reaction when tapped below the kneecap.

Question 2

What is a neuron? Explain its structure.
Solution:
A neuron is the structural and functional unit of the nervous system. It helps in transmitting nerve impulses.

Main parts:
• Dendrites: Receive signals.
• Cell body: Contains nucleus.
• Axon: Transmits signals away from the cell body.
• Axon terminals: Pass signals to the next neuron or effector.

Question 3

What is the role of the brain in coordination?
Solution:
The brain receives information from sense organs, processes it, and sends signals to muscles and glands. It controls voluntary actions, balance, memory, and thinking.

Question 4

What is a synapse?
Solution:
A synapse is the junction between two neurons. It helps in transmitting nerve impulses chemically through neurotransmitters.

Question 5

What happens at a synapse when an impulse reaches the axon terminal?
Solution:
The axon terminal releases chemical neurotransmitters which cross the synaptic gap and generate an impulse in the next neuron.

Question 6

How are involuntary actions different from voluntary actions?
Solution:
Voluntary actions: Performed with conscious control (e.g., writing, walking).
Involuntary actions: Occur automatically without conscious control (e.g., heartbeat, breathing).

Question 7

Name some endocrine glands in humans and the hormones they secrete.
Solution:
• Pituitary – Growth hormone
• Thyroid – Thyroxine
• Pancreas – Insulin
• Adrenal – Adrenaline
• Testes – Testosterone
• Ovaries – Estrogen

Question 8

How is coordination in plants different from animals?
Solution:
Plants use chemical hormones (e.g., auxin) for coordination, which is slower. Animals have both nervous and endocrine systems, making responses faster and more controlled.

Exercises

Question 9

Which of the following is not controlled by the brain?
(a) Thinking
(b) Heartbeat
(c) Balance of the body
(d) Writing
Solution:
(b) Heartbeat.
It is controlled by the medulla (brainstem) and autonomic nervous system.

Question 10

How do plants respond to stimuli?
Solution:
Plants respond to stimuli through **tropisms** (growth movements) and **hormones** such as auxin. Example: Bending of plant towards sunlight (phototropism).

Question 11

What are plant hormones? Name four plant hormones.
Solution:
Plant hormones are chemical substances that regulate plant growth and responses.

Four hormones:
• Auxins
• Gibberellins
• Cytokinins
• Abscisic acid (ABA)

Question 12

Explain the structure and function of the human brain.
Solution:
The brain has three regions:

1. Forebrain: Controls thinking, memory, senses.
2. Midbrain: Controls reflex movements of head and eyes.
3. Hindbrain: Includes cerebellum (balance) and medulla (heartbeat, breathing).

It is protected by the skull and three membranes called meninges.

Question 13

Distinguish between sensory, motor, and association neurons.
Solution:
Sensory neurons: Carry impulses from receptors to CNS.
Motor neurons: Carry impulses from CNS to effectors.
Association neurons: Connect sensory and motor neurons within the CNS.
