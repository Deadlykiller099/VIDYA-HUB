NCERT Solutions for Class 10 Science Chapter 14: Our Environment

In-Text Questions

Question 1

What are the components of our environment?
Solution:
The environment consists of **biotic components** (living organisms) and **abiotic components** (non-living things like air, water, soil, sunlight, and temperature).

Question 2

Define ecosystem.
Solution:
An **ecosystem** is a functional unit of nature where **living organisms interact with each other and with abiotic components** of the environment.

Question 3

Give one example of an aquatic ecosystem and a terrestrial ecosystem.
Solution:
- Aquatic ecosystem: **Pond** or **Lake** - Terrestrial ecosystem: **Forest** or **Grassland**

Question 4

What is the role of decomposers in the environment?
Solution:
Decomposers like bacteria and fungi **break down dead plants and animals**, recycling nutrients back into the soil for plants to use.

Question 5

What is biodiversity?
Solution:
**Biodiversity** is the **variety of life forms** in a particular habitat or on Earth. It includes diversity of species, genes, and ecosystems.

Exercise Questions

Question 1

Define environment. Name its main components.
Solution:
The **environment** is the sum total of **all external conditions** that affect an organism. Components: - **Abiotic:** Air, water, soil, sunlight, temperature - **Biotic:** Plants, animals, microbes

Question 2

What is an ecosystem? Give an example.
Solution:
An **ecosystem** is a system in which living organisms interact with each other and with their physical environment. Example: **Pond ecosystem**, **forest ecosystem**.

Question 3

What do you mean by food chain? Illustrate with an example.
Solution:
A **food chain** shows the sequence of organisms in which **energy and nutrients pass from one organism to another**. Example: Grass → Grasshopper → Frog → Snake → Eagle

Question 4

Define food web. How is it different from a food chain?
Solution:
A **food web** is a network of **interconnected food chains** in an ecosystem. Difference: A food chain is linear, while a food web is complex and shows multiple interactions.

Question 5

What are producers, consumers, and decomposers? Give examples.
Solution:
- **Producers:** Make their own food (e.g., plants) - **Consumers:** Eat producers or other consumers (e.g., herbivores, carnivores) - **Decomposers:** Break down dead matter (e.g., fungi, bacteria)

Question 6

Explain the importance of decomposers in an ecosystem.
Solution:
Decomposers **recycle nutrients** by breaking down dead plants and animals, maintaining soil fertility and sustaining the ecosystem.

Question 7

What is ecological balance? How can human activities disturb it?
Solution:
**Ecological balance** is the **stable relationship between organisms and their environment**. Human activities like **deforestation, pollution, overhunting, and industrialization** can disturb it.

Question 8

List any two measures to protect our environment.
Solution:
1. Planting more **trees and conserving forests** 2. Reducing **pollution and recycling waste**

Question 9

What is meant by biodiversity conservation?
Solution:
**Biodiversity conservation** is the **protection and sustainable use of plants, animals, and other organisms** in their natural habitats.

Question 10

Explain the consequences of deforestation.
Solution:
Consequences: - Loss of biodiversity - Soil erosion - Climate change and global warming - Disruption of ecological balance

Question 11

Name two environmental problems caused by human activities.
Solution:
1. Air and water pollution 2. Deforestation

Question 12

What is the role of forests in maintaining ecological balance?
Solution:
Forests provide **oxygen, shelter, and food**, prevent **soil erosion**, maintain **water cycle**, and **regulate climate**, sustaining ecological balance.
