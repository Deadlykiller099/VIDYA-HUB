NCERT Solutions for Class 10 Science

Chapter 1: Chemical Reactions and Equations (English Medium)

In-Text Questions (Page 6)

Question 1

Why should a magnesium ribbon be cleaned before burning in air?
Solution:
Magnesium ribbon should be cleaned before burning in air because a layer of magnesium oxide forms on its surface due to reaction with oxygen in air. This layer hinders the burning of magnesium ribbon.

Question 2

Write the balanced equation for the following reaction and identify the type of reaction:
Magnesium + dil. Hydrochloric acid → Magnesium chloride + Water
Solution:
Balanced Equation: Mg + 2HCl → MgCl₂ + H₂
Type: Displacement reaction.

Question 3

A student writes the following incorrect balanced equation:
Mg + 2HCl → MgCl₂ + H₂O
Explain the following in terms of observation he must have made to come to such a conclusion.
Solution:
The student must have observed that heat is evolved during the reaction, which is a characteristic of exothermic reaction. He confused it with water being formed, but actually hydrogen gas is evolved.

In-Text Questions (Page 13)

Question 1

A shiny brown-coloured element ‘X’ on heating in air becomes black in colour. Name the element ‘X’ and the black-coloured compound formed. What type of reaction is it?
Solution:
Element ‘X’ is copper (Cu). Black-coloured compound is copper oxide (CuO).
Reaction: 2Cu + O₂ → 2CuO (Oxidation reaction).

Question 2

Why is the amount of gas collected in one of the test tubes in Activity 1.7 double of the amount collected in the other? Name this gas.
Solution:
Hydrogen gas is evolved twice as fast at the cathode as oxygen gas at the anode because water contains two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom (H₂O). The gas is hydrogen.

Question 2

Balance the following chemical equations:
(a) HNO₃ + Ca(OH)₂ → Ca(NO₃)₂ + H₂O
(b) NaOH + H₂SO₄ → Na₂SO₄ + H₂O
(c) NaCl + AgNO₃ → AgCl + NaNO₃
(d) BaCl₂ + H₂SO₄ → BaSO₄ + HCl
Solution:
(a) 2HNO₃ + Ca(OH)₂ → Ca(NO₃)₂ + 2H₂O
(b) 2NaOH + H₂SO₄ → Na₂SO₄ + 2H₂O
(c) Already balanced.
(d) BaCl₂ + H₂SO₄ → BaSO₄ + 2HCl

Question 3

Write the balanced chemical equations for the following reactions:
(a) Sodium carbonate on reaction with hydrochloric acid in equal molar concentrations gives sodium chloride and sodium hydrogencarbonate.
(b) Sodium hydrogencarbonate on reaction with hydrochloric acid gives sodium chloride, water and liberates carbon dioxide.
Solution:
(a) Na₂CO₃ + HCl → NaCl + NaHCO₃
(b) NaHCO₃ + HCl → NaCl + H₂O + CO₂

Question 4

What is a balanced chemical equation? Why should chemical equations be balanced?
Solution:
A balanced chemical equation has an equal number of atoms of different elements in the reactants and products.
Equations must be balanced to satisfy the law of conservation of mass.

Question 5(a)

Translate the following statements into chemical equations and then balance them:
Hydrogen gas combines with nitrogen to form ammonia.
Solution:
3H₂ + N₂ → 2NH₃

Question 5(b)

Hydrogen sulphide gas burns in air to give water and sulphur dioxide.
Solution:
2H₂S + 3O₂ → 2H₂O + 2SO₂

Question 5(c)

Barium chloride reacts with aluminium sulphate to give aluminium chloride and a precipitate of barium sulphate.
Solution:
3BaCl₂ + Al₂(SO₄)₃ → 2AlCl₃ + 3BaSO₄

Question 5(d)

Potassium metal reacts with water to give potassium hydroxide and hydrogen gas.
Solution:
2K + 2H₂O → 2KOH + H₂

Question 6

Balance the following reactions:
(a) Na + O₂ → Na₂O
(b) H₂O → H₂ + O₂
(c) Fe + H₂O → Fe₃O₄ + H₂
(d) AlCl₃ + Na → NaCl + Al
(e) H₂ + O₂ → H₂O
(f) NH₃ + O₂ → NO + H₂O
(g) Ca(OH)₂ + CO₂ → CaCO₃ + H₂O
(h) Zn + HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂
Solution:
(a) 4Na + O₂ → 2Na₂O
(b) 2H₂O → 2H₂ + O₂
(c) 3Fe + 4H₂O → Fe₃O₄ + 4H₂
(d) 2AlCl₃ + 6Na → 6NaCl + 2Al
(e) 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O
(f) 4NH₃ + 5O₂ → 4NO + 6H₂O
(g) Already balanced.
(h) Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂

Question 7(a)

What is an activity series of metals or reactivity series of metals?
Solution:
It is a series of metals arranged in decreasing order of their reactivity.

Question 7(b)

Why is the reactivity series useful?
Solution:
It helps to predict the nature of the chemical reaction of a metal with a given salt solution or water.

Question 8

Can two different atomic species of elements have the same atomic mass? If so, name them. Can two different atomic species of elements have the same mass number? If so, name them.
Solution:
Yes, two different elements can have the same atomic mass (e.g., Argon and Calcium both ≈40 u).
Yes, isotopes have the same mass number (e.g., Carbon-12 and Carbon-13).

Question 9

Which of the following is a displacement reaction?
(i) CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂
(ii) 2NaCl → 2Na + Cl₂
(iii) Fe + CuSO₄ → FeSO₄ + Cu
(iv) 2Cu + O₂ → 2CuO
Solution:
(iii) Fe + CuSO₄ → FeSO₄ + Cu

Question 10

In the refining of silver, the recovery of silver from silver nitrate solution involved displacement reaction by copper metal. Write down the reaction involved.
Solution:
Cu + 2AgNO₃ → Cu(NO₃)₂ + 2Ag

Question 11

What do you mean by exothermic and endothermic reactions? Give examples.
Solution:
Exothermic: Reactions that release heat (e.g., CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O + Heat).
Endothermic: Reactions that absorb heat (e.g., 2NH₃ + Heat → N₂ + 3H₂).

Question 12

Why is respiration considered an exothermic reaction? Explain.
Solution:
During respiration, glucose combines with oxygen to form carbon dioxide and water, releasing energy as heat. Hence, it is exothermic.

Question 13

Write the balanced chemical equation for the following and identify the type of reaction:
(a) Zinc reacts with dilute sulphuric acid to form zinc sulphate and hydrogen gas.
(b) Sodium hydroxide reacts with dilute hydrochloric acid to form sodium chloride and water.
(c) Magnesium reacts with oxygen to form magnesium oxide.
Solution:
(a) Zn + H₂SO₄ → ZnSO₄ + H₂ (Displacement)
(b) NaOH + HCl → NaCl + H₂O (Neutralisation)
(c) 2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO (Combination)

Question 14

What is a precipitation reaction? Give examples.
Solution:
A reaction where an insoluble solid (precipitate) forms from a solution.
Example: AgNO₃ + NaCl → AgCl↓ + NaNO₃

Question 15

Explain the following terms with one example each:
(a) Corrosion
(b) Rancidity
Solution:
(a) Corrosion: Slow oxidation of metals in air (e.g., rusting of iron: 4Fe + 3O₂ + 6H₂O → 2Fe₂O₃.xH₂O).
(b) Rancidity: Oxidation of fats/oils in food, causing bad smell/taste. Prevented by adding antioxidants.
