NCERT Solutions for Class 10 Science Chapter 5: Life Processes (English Medium)

In-Text Questions

Question 1

Why is diffusion insufficient to meet the oxygen requirements of multicellular organisms like humans?
Solution:
Diffusion is a slow process and cannot deliver oxygen efficiently to all cells in a large, multicellular body. Specialized respiratory and circulatory systems are required.

Question 2

What criteria do we use to decide whether something is alive?
Solution:
Life processes like nutrition, respiration, excretion, reproduction, and growth distinguish living organisms from non-living things.

Question 3

What are outside raw materials used by an organism?
Solution:
Food, water, and oxygen are the main outside raw materials required for life processes.

Question 4

What processes would you consider essential for maintaining life?
Solution:
Nutrition, respiration, transportation, and excretion are essential processes that maintain life.

Question 5

What are the differences between autotrophic and heterotrophic nutrition?
Solution:
Autotrophic NutritionHeterotrophic Nutrition
Organisms prepare their own food using CO₂ and water.Organisms depend on others for food.
Occurs mainly in green plants.Occurs in animals and many microorganisms.

Question 6

Where do plants get each of the raw materials required for photosynthesis?
Solution:
CO₂ from air through stomata; water from soil through roots; sunlight from the sun; chlorophyll from chloroplasts.

Question 7

What is the role of the acid in our stomach?
Solution:
Stomach acid (HCl) kills bacteria and creates an acidic medium for the enzyme pepsin to digest proteins.

Question 8

How do digestive enzymes act?
Solution:
Digestive enzymes break complex food molecules into simpler, absorbable forms.

Question 9

What is the role of villi in the small intestine?
Solution:
Villi increase surface area for absorption of digested food.

Question 10

What advantage does a terrestrial organism have over an aquatic organism in obtaining oxygen?
Solution:
Air contains more oxygen than water, so terrestrial organisms obtain oxygen more easily.

Question 11

How is glucose oxidized in different organisms?
Solution:
• Aerobic respiration → CO₂ + water + energy
• Anaerobic respiration (yeast) → alcohol + CO₂
• Anaerobic respiration (muscles) → lactic acid + energy

Question 12

Why is it necessary to separate oxygenated and deoxygenated blood in mammals and birds?
Solution:
It ensures efficient oxygen supply and maintains constant body temperature (warm-blooded nature).

Question 13

List three characteristics of lungs that make them efficient for gas exchange.
Solution:
• Thin walls
• Rich blood supply
• Large surface area due to alveoli

Question 14

What are the components of the human transport system?
Solution:
Heart, blood, and blood vessels.

Question 15

What are the components of the transport system in plants?
Solution:
Xylem (water and minerals) and phloem (food).

Question 16

How are water and minerals transported in plants?
Solution:
Through xylem by transpiration pull, root pressure, and capillary action.

Question 17

How is food transported in plants?
Solution:
Through phloem by active transport using ATP (called translocation).

Question 18

Describe the structure and function of a nephron.
Solution:
A nephron has Bowman's capsule, tubules, and a collecting duct. It filters blood, reabsorbs useful substances, and forms urine.

Question 19

How do plants get rid of waste products?
Solution:
Through transpiration, storage in leaves/gums/resins, and diffusion via stomata.

Question 20

How is urine production regulated?
Solution:
Urine amount depends on water level, ADH hormone, and body’s hydration needs.

Exercise Questions

Question 21

The kidneys in human beings are a part of the system for
(a) Nutrition
(b) Respiration
(c) Excretion
(d) Transportation
Solution:
(c) Excretion

Question 22

The xylem in plants is responsible for
(a) Transport of water
(b) Transport of food
(c) Transport of amino acids
(d) Transport of oxygen
Solution:
(a) Transport of water

Question 23

The autotrophic mode of nutrition requires
(a) CO₂ and water
(b) Chlorophyll
(c) Sunlight
(d) All of the above
Solution:
(d) All of the above

Question 24

The breakdown of pyruvate to CO₂ and water requires
(a) Cytoplasm
(b) Mitochondria
(c) Chloroplast
