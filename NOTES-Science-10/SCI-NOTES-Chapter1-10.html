Class 10 Science Notes: Chapter 1 - Chemical Reactions and Equations

1. Introduction to Chemical Reactions
  • A chemical reaction is a process in which substances called **reactants** are transformed into **products**.
  • General form: Reactants → Products
  • Indicators of chemical reactions include:
    • Change in color
    • Formation of gas
    • Formation of precipitate
    • Temperature change (heat absorbed or released)
    • Emission of light
Example: 2H2 + O2 → 2H2O
2. Types of Chemical Reactions
2.1 Combination Reactions
  • Two or more reactants combine to form a single product.
  • General form: A + B → AB
  • Example: 2Mg + O2 → 2MgO
2.2 Decomposition Reactions
  • A single reactant breaks down into two or more products.
  • General form: AB → A + B
  • Example: 2HgO → 2Hg + O2
2.3 Displacement Reactions
  • A more reactive element displaces a less reactive element from its compound.
  • General form: A + BC → AC + B
  • Example: Zn + CuSO4 → ZnSO4 + Cu
2.4 Double Displacement Reactions
  • Exchange of ions between two compounds.
  • General form: AB + CD → AD + CB
  • Example: AgNO3 + NaCl → AgCl↓ + NaNO3
2.5 Redox Reactions
  • Oxidation: loss of electrons or gain of oxygen
  • Reduction: gain of electrons or loss of oxygen
  • Redox: oxidation and reduction occur simultaneously
  • Example: Zn + CuSO4 → ZnSO4 + Cu
3. Writing and Balancing Chemical Equations
  • Reactants are written on the left, products on the right separated by →.
  • Equations must obey the **Law of Conservation of Mass**.
  • Tips for balancing:
    • Balance atoms of one element at a time.
    • Start with complex molecules.
    • Check all atoms at the end.
Example: Unbalanced: H2 + O2 → H2O
Balanced: 2H2 + O2 → 2H2O
4. Exothermic and Endothermic Reactions
  • Exothermic: Releases heat/light energy. Example: Combustion of fuels, respiration.
  • Endothermic: Absorbs heat energy. Example: Photosynthesis, decomposition of CaCO3.
5. Indicators of Chemical Reactions
  • Change in color (e.g., bleaching of litmus)
  • Evolution of gas (e.g., HCl + Zn → H2)
  • Formation of precipitate (e.g., AgNO3 + NaCl → AgCl↓)
  • Temperature change (heat absorbed or released)
  • Emission of light (e.g., burning Mg)
6. Quick Revision Tips
  • Identify **reactants and products** first.
  • Check for **energy changes** (heat/light/gas formation).
  • Balance equations carefully using **LCM method**.
  • Know all **types of reactions** with examples.
  • Practice NCERT examples and exercises for clarity.
