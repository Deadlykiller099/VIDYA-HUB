Class 10 Science Notes: Chapter 4 - Carbon and its Compounds

1. Introduction

Carbon is a non-metal with unique properties such as catenation (ability to form long chains) and tetravalency (forms four bonds). Carbon compounds are classified as organic and inorganic compounds.

2. Covalent Bonding in Carbon
  • Carbon atoms form **covalent bonds** to achieve a stable octet.
  • Each carbon atom forms **four covalent bonds** due to its tetravalency.
  • Catenation allows carbon to form chains, branched structures, and rings.
3. Saturated and Unsaturated Hydrocarbons
  • Saturated Hydrocarbons: Contain single bonds only (alkanes). General formula: CnH2n+2. Example: CH4, C2H6
  • Unsaturated Hydrocarbons: Contain double or triple bonds (alkenes & alkynes). Example: C2H4 (ethylene), C2H2 (acetylene)
4. Functional Groups

Functional groups are specific atoms or groups of atoms that determine the properties of organic compounds.

  • -OH : Alcohols (ethanol, CH3CH2OH)
  • -CHO : Aldehydes (formaldehyde, HCHO)
  • -COOH : Carboxylic acids (acetic acid, CH3COOH)
  • -NH2 : Amines (methylamine, CH3NH2)
5. Homologous Series
  • A homologous series is a group of organic compounds with the same functional group, similar chemical properties, and successive members differing by CH2.
  • Example: Alkanes – CH4, C2H6, C3H8
6. Chemical Properties of Carbon Compounds
  • Combustion: Carbon compounds burn in oxygen to produce CO2 and water, releasing energy.
  • Oxidation: Alcohols can be oxidized to aldehydes, ketones, or acids.
  • Substitution Reaction: Halogens replace hydrogen in alkanes (e.g., CH4 + Cl2 → CH3Cl + HCl).
  • Addition Reaction: Unsaturated compounds react with halogens or hydrogen (e.g., C2H4 + Br2 → C2H4Br2).
7. Alcohols, Aldehydes, and Carboxylic Acids
  • Alcohols: Contain -OH group, e.g., ethanol.
  • Aldehydes: Contain -CHO group, e.g., formaldehyde.
  • Carboxylic acids: Contain -COOH group, e.g., acetic acid.
  • Important reactions: oxidation, esterification (acid + alcohol → ester + water).
8. Soaps and Detergents
  • Soaps: Sodium or potassium salts of fatty acids, form micelles to remove grease.
  • Detergents: Sulfonate salts, effective in hard water.
  • Uses: Cleaning, washing, removing oil/grease.
9. Uses of Carbon Compounds
  • Fuel: C, hydrocarbons, alcohols
  • Medicines: organic compounds as drugs
  • Polymers: plastics, synthetic fibers
  • Solvents: alcohols, ketones
10. Quick Revision Tips
  • Remember tetravalency and catenation of carbon
  • Know functional groups and their reactions
  • Distinguish between saturated & unsaturated compounds
  • Practice combustion, substitution, and addition reactions
