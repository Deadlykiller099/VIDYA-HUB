Class 10 Science Notes: Chapter 10 - The Human Eye and Colourful World

1. Introduction

This chapter explains the structure and working of the human eye, defects of vision, and phenomena related to light such as dispersion, scattering, and formation of the spectrum.

2. Structure of Human Eye
  • Parts of the eye:
    • Cornea: transparent outer covering; refracts light
    • Iris: controls the amount of light entering the eye
    • Lens: focuses light on the retina
    • Retina: contains photoreceptor cells (rods and cones)
    • Optic nerve: carries impulses from retina to brain
    • Conjunctiva: protects and lubricates the eye
    • Ciliary muscles: change shape of lens for near/far vision
3. Defects of Vision and Corrections
  • Myopia (nearsightedness): Cannot see distant objects; corrected by concave lens
  • Hypermetropia (farsightedness): Cannot see near objects; corrected by convex lens
  • Presbyopia: Age-related difficulty in near vision; corrected by bifocal lens
  • Cataract: Clouding of the lens; treated surgically
4. Refraction and Lenses
  • Convex lens: converging lens; can form real or virtual images
  • Concave lens: diverging lens; forms virtual, erect, and diminished images
  • Lens formula: 1/f = 1/v - 1/u
  • Magnification: m = hi/ho = v/u
5. Dispersion of Light
  • White light splits into seven colors (VIBGYOR) when passed through a prism.
  • Red bends least, violet bends most.
  • Used in formation of rainbow.
6. Atmospheric Refraction and Scattering
  • Refraction by atmosphere causes:
    • Apparent position of sun rises earlier and sets later
    • Twinkling of stars
  • Scattering of light:
    • Blue light scattered more → sky appears blue
    • Red light scattered more during sunrise/sunset → sun appears red
7. Quick Revision Tips
  • Learn human eye structure and functions of each part.
  • Remember defects of vision and their lens corrections.
  • Revise lens formulas and magnification rules.
  • Understand dispersion, rainbow formation, atmospheric refraction, and scattering.
