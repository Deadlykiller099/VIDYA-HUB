Class 10 Science Notes: Chapter - Electricity

1. Introduction

Electricity is the flow of electric charge through a conductor. It is used to power electrical devices and is governed by laws such as Ohm's law.

2. Electric Current
  • Definition: Rate of flow of charge through a conductor.
  • Formula: I = Q / t
    • I = Current (Amperes)
    • Q = Charge (Coulombs)
    • t = Time (seconds)
  • Unit: Ampere (A)
3. Ohm's Law
  • Statement: Current through a conductor is directly proportional to voltage across it, provided temperature remains constant.
  • Formula: V = I × R
    • V = Voltage (Volts)
    • I = Current (Amperes)
    • R = Resistance (Ohms)
  • Resistance of a conductor: R = ρ × L / A
    • ρ = Resistivity of material
    • L = Length of conductor
    • A = Cross-sectional area
4. Series and Parallel Circuits
  • Series Circuit: Components connected end-to-end.
    • Total resistance: Rtotal = R1 + R2 + ...
    • Current same through all components
    • Voltage divides among components
  • Parallel Circuit: Components connected across same potential difference.
    • Total resistance: 1/Rtotal = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + ...
    • Voltage same across each component
    • Current divides among branches
5. Electric Power
  • Definition: Rate at which electrical energy is converted into other forms of energy.
  • Formula: P = V × I = I² × R = V² / R
  • Unit: Watt (W)
  • Energy consumed: E = P × t (in Joules or kWh)
6. Heating Effect of Current
  • When current flows through a conductor, it produces heat.
  • Formula: H = I² × R × t
  • Applications: Electric heater, electric bulb, fuse, etc.
7. Domestic Electric Circuits
  • Use of fuses to prevent overcurrent
  • Safety measures: Earthing, MCB (Miniature Circuit Breaker), proper insulation
  • Wiring types: Series for lights, parallel for appliances
8. Quick Revision Tips
  • Remember Ohm’s law and its formulas
  • Know series vs parallel circuit rules
  • Revise power, energy, and heating effect formulas
  • Understand domestic circuit components and safety devices
