Class 10 Science Notes: Chapter 5 - Life Processes

1. Introduction

Life processes are the processes that sustain life in living organisms. They include nutrition, respiration, transportation, and excretion. These processes help organisms grow, reproduce, and maintain homeostasis.

2. Nutrition
  • Nutrition is the process by which organisms obtain and utilize food to release energy and build body components.
  • Types of nutrition:
    • Autotrophic: Organisms synthesize their own food (e.g., plants via photosynthesis).
    • Heterotrophic: Organisms depend on others for food (e.g., humans, animals, fungi).
  • Photosynthesis: Process by which green plants make food using sunlight, CO2, and water.
3. Respiration
  • Respiration is the process of breaking down glucose to release energy.
  • Types:
    • Aerobic: Requires oxygen; occurs in mitochondria; produces CO2, H2O, and energy.
    • Anaerobic: Does not require oxygen; occurs in cytoplasm; produces lactic acid (in animals) or ethanol + CO2 (in yeast).
  • Respiration provides energy for growth, repair, movement, and other life processes.
4. Transportation
  • Transportation is the movement of substances such as water, nutrients, and gases in organisms.
  • In humans:
    • Blood transports oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and waste products.
    • Heart pumps blood through arteries, veins, and capillaries.
  • In plants:
    • Xylem transports water and minerals from roots to leaves.
    • Phloem transports food from leaves to other parts of the plant.
5. Excretion
  • Excretion is the removal of metabolic waste products from the body.
  • In humans: kidneys filter blood to remove urea, salts, and excess water as urine.
  • Other excretory organs: skin (sweat), lungs (CO2), liver (detoxification).
  • In plants: excretion occurs via stomata (gases), resin, gum, and leaf fall.
6. Quick Revision Tips
  • Remember the four life processes: nutrition, respiration, transportation, excretion.
  • Know examples of autotrophic and heterotrophic nutrition.
  • Differentiate between aerobic and anaerobic respiration.
  • Understand plant and human transportation systems.
  • Learn human excretory organs and plant excretion methods.
