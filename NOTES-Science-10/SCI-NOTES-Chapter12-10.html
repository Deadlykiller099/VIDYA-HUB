Class 10 Science Notes: Chapter - Magnetic Effects of Current

1. Introduction

Electric current produces a magnetic field around it. This forms the basis of electromagnetism, which is widely used in electric devices.

2. Magnetic Field and Field Lines
  • Magnetic field: Region around a magnet or current-carrying conductor where magnetic force is experienced.
  • Field lines: Represent the direction and strength of magnetic field (from N to S).
  • Field due to straight conductor: Circular field around conductor; direction by right-hand rule.
3. Magnetic Field due to Current-Carrying Conductor
  • Straight conductor: Field is concentric circles; magnitude B ∝ I / r
  • Solenoid: Coil of wire; produces uniform magnetic field inside
  • Right-hand rule: Thumb → current, Fingers → field direction
4. Force on Current-Carrying Conductor in a Magnetic Field
  • Current in a magnetic field experiences a force
  • Direction given by Fleming's Left-Hand Rule: Thumb → motion, Forefinger → magnetic field, Middle finger → current
  • Applications: Electric motor, loudspeakers
5. Electromagnetic Induction
  • Definition: Production of induced current in a coil due to changing magnetic field
  • Applications: Electric generators, transformers, induction cookers
  • Fleming’s Right-Hand Rule: Thumb → motion of conductor, Forefinger → field, Middle finger → induced current
6. Electric Motor
  • Device converting electrical energy into mechanical energy
  • Uses force on current-carrying conductor in magnetic field
  • Components: Armature, brushes, magnetic field, commutator
7. Electromagnet
  • Coil of wire carrying current around iron core
  • Can be switched on/off; strength increases with number of turns and current
  • Applications: Lifting heavy magnetic objects, electric bells, relays
8. Quick Revision Tips
  • Right-hand rule for field around conductor/solenoid
  • Fleming’s left-hand rule for force on conductor
  • Fleming’s right-hand rule for induced current
  • Know applications: Motor, generator, electromagnet, transformer
