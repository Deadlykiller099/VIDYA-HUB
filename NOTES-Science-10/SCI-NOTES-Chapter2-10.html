Class 10 Science Notes: Chapter 2 - Acids, Bases, and Salts

1. Introduction
  • Acids, bases, and salts are important chemical compounds with everyday applications.
  • Acids: substances that release H+ ions in solution.
  • Bases: substances that release OH- ions in solution.
  • Salts: compounds formed from neutralization of acids by bases.
2. Properties of Acids
  • Sour taste (e.g., lemon, vinegar)
  • Turn blue litmus red
  • React with metals to produce hydrogen gas: Zn + HCl → ZnCl2 + H2
  • React with bases to form salt and water (neutralization)
  • Conduct electricity (electrolytes)
3. Properties of Bases
  • Bitter taste (e.g., soap, baking soda)
  • Turn red litmus blue
  • React with acids to form salt and water
  • Conduct electricity if soluble in water (electrolytes)
4. Strong and Weak Acids/Bases
  • Strong acids: Completely ionize in water (e.g., HCl, H2SO4)
  • Weak acids: Partially ionize in water (e.g., CH3COOH)
  • Strong bases: Completely dissociate in water (e.g., NaOH, KOH)
  • Weak bases: Partially dissociate (e.g., NH4OH)
5. Neutralization
  • Neutralization is the reaction between an acid and a base to produce salt and water.
  • General reaction: Acid + Base → Salt + Water
Example: HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H2O
6. Salts
  • Salts are formed when the H+ of acids is replaced by a metal or ammonium ion.
  • Examples:
    • NaCl (from HCl + NaOH)
    • CaSO4 (from H2SO4 + Ca(OH)2)
  • Salts have various uses: NaCl for cooking, CaCO3 in construction, NaHCO3 in baking.
7. Acid-Base Reactions in Daily Life
  • Antacids neutralize excess stomach acid (Al(OH)3, Mg(OH)2)
  • Cleaning products often contain bases (soap, detergents)
  • Vinegar (acetic acid) used in food preservation
  • Lime (CaO) neutralizes acidic soil
8. pH Scale
  • Measures the acidity or basicity of a solution.
  • pH < 7: acidic; pH = 7: neutral; pH > 7: basic
  • Strong acids: pH 0-3; Weak acids: pH 4-6
  • Strong bases: pH 12-14; Weak bases: pH 8-11
9. Quick Revision Tips
  • Remember acid/base indicators: litmus, phenolphthalein, methyl orange
  • Know common strong and weak acids/bases
  • Practice writing neutralization reactions
  • Associate everyday uses with chemical reactions for better memory
