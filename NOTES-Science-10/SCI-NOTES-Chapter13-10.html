Class 10 Science Notes: Chapter 13 - Our Environment

1. Introduction

Our environment is the combination of all living and non-living components around us. It includes air, water, soil, plants, animals, and human beings, and their interactions.

2. Components of Environment
  • Abiotic components: Non-living components like sunlight, water, air, soil, temperature.
  • Biotic components: Living components like plants, animals, humans, microorganisms.
3. Ecosystem
  • Definition: A functional unit of nature where living organisms interact with each other and their environment.
  • Components:
    • Producers: Green plants (autotrophs)
    • Consumers: Herbivores, carnivores, omnivores
    • Decomposers: Fungi, bacteria
  • Energy flow: Sun → Producers → Consumers → Decomposers
  • Food chain: Sequence of organisms showing transfer of energy
  • Food web: Interconnected food chains
4. Environmental Problems
  • Deforestation: Loss of forests; affects oxygen, biodiversity, and climate
  • Pollution:
    • Air pollution: Smoke, CO2, SO2, global warming
    • Water pollution: Industrial effluents, sewage, pesticides
    • Soil pollution: Chemical fertilizers, pesticides, industrial waste
    • Noise pollution: Loudspeakers, vehicles
  • Global warming: Rise in Earth's temperature due to greenhouse gases (CO2, CH4)
  • Ozone depletion: Thinning of ozone layer by CFCs, leading to harmful UV radiation
5. Conservation of Environment
  • Plant trees and maintain forests
  • Reduce, reuse, recycle waste
  • Use renewable sources of energy (solar, wind)
  • Control pollution: Use cleaner fuels, treat sewage
  • Protect biodiversity and wildlife
6. Ozone Layer and Greenhouse Effect
  • Ozone layer: Protects Earth from harmful UV rays; depletion causes skin cancer, cataract.
  • Greenhouse effect: Trapping of heat by gases like CO2, CH4, leading to global warming.
7. Quick Revision Tips
  • Understand ecosystem components and energy flow
  • Remember major environmental problems and their causes
  • Know ways to conserve environment and reduce pollution
  • Revise ozone layer depletion and greenhouse effect
