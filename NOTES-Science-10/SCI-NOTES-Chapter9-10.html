Class 10 Science Notes: Chapter 9 - Light: Reflection and Refraction

1. Introduction

Light is a form of energy that enables us to see objects. It travels in straight lines and exhibits reflection, refraction, and dispersion. This chapter focuses on the laws of reflection and refraction, mirrors, lenses, and human eye defects.

2. Reflection of Light
  • Reflection is the bouncing back of light from a surface.
  • Laws of Reflection:
    • Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection
    • Incident ray, reflected ray, and normal lie in the same plane
  • Types of Reflection:
    • Regular (specular) reflection – smooth surfaces (plane mirror)
    • Diffuse reflection – rough surfaces
  • Images formed by plane mirrors:
    • Virtual, erect, laterally inverted, same size as object
3. Spherical Mirrors
  • Two types: Concave and Convex
  • Concave mirror:
    • Converging mirror
    • Can form real or virtual images depending on object position
  • Convex mirror:
    • Diverging mirror
    • Always forms virtual, erect, and diminished image
  • Mirror formula: 1/f = 1/v + 1/u
  • Magnification: m = hi/ho = -v/u
4. Refraction of Light
  • Refraction is the bending of light when it passes from one medium to another.
  • Laws of Refraction:
    • Incident ray, refracted ray, and normal lie in the same plane
    • The ratio of sine of angle of incidence to sine of angle of refraction is constant (Snell’s law)
  • Refractive index (μ) = speed of light in vacuum / speed of light in medium
  • Apparent depth: Objects appear shallower in water due to refraction
5. Spherical Lenses
  • Two types: Convex (converging) and Concave (diverging)
  • Convex lens:
    • Can form real or virtual images depending on object distance
    • Used in magnifying glasses, cameras, and microscopes
  • Concave lens:
    • Always forms virtual, erect, and diminished images
    • Used in spectacles for myopia
  • Lens formula: 1/f = 1/v - 1/u
  • Magnification: m = hi/ho = v/u
6. Human Eye and Defects
  • Eye is an optical instrument that forms images on the retina.
  • Defects of vision:
    • Myopia (nearsightedness) – corrected by concave lens
    • Hypermetropia (farsightedness) – corrected by convex lens
    • Cataract – clouding of lens; treated surgically
    • Presbyopia – age-related difficulty in near vision; corrected by bifocal lens
  • Power of accommodation: Eye’s ability to focus on near and distant objects.
7. Refraction through Prism
  • Prism bends light due to refraction at its surfaces.
  • Dispersion: Splitting of white light into its constituent colors (spectrum).
  • Rainbow is formed due to dispersion, reflection, and refraction of sunlight in water droplets.
8. Quick Revision Tips
  • Learn laws of reflection and refraction clearly.
  • Know mirror and lens formulas and sign conventions.
  • Remember differences between concave/convex mirrors and lenses.
  • Understand human eye defects and their corrections.
  • Revise refraction through prism and dispersion of light.
