Class 10 Science Notes: Chapter 7 - Reproduction

1. Introduction

Reproduction is the biological process by which living organisms produce new individuals of the same species, ensuring survival of species.

2. Types of Reproduction
  • Asexual Reproduction: Single parent produces offspring identical to itself.
    • Binary fission: Amoeba, Bacteria
    • Budding: Hydra, Yeast
    • Fragmentation: Spirogyra, Planaria
    • Spore formation: Fungi (Mucor, Rhizopus)
  • Sexual Reproduction: Involves fusion of male and female gametes.
    • Occurs in flowering plants and animals.
3. Reproduction in Plants
  • Asexual: Vegetative propagation
    • Runners: Grass
    • Stem cutting: Rose
    • Tubers: Potato
    • Bulbs: Onion
  • Sexual: Flower reproduction
    • Pollination: Transfer of pollen to stigma
    • Fertilization: Fusion of male and female gametes
    • Seed formation and germination
4. Reproduction in Animals
  • Asexual: Binary fission (Amoeba), Budding (Hydra)
  • Sexual:
    • External fertilization: Fish, Amphibians
    • Internal fertilization: Mammals, Birds
5. Human Reproduction
  • Male Reproductive System: Testes, sperm, vas deferens, urethra, penis.
  • Female Reproductive System: Ovaries, ovum, fallopian tube, uterus, vagina.
  • Fertilization: Fusion of sperm and ovum → zygote.
  • Embryonic Development: Zygote → embryo → fetus → birth.
6. Adolescence and Reproductive Health
  • Puberty: Age when reproductive organs mature.
  • Secondary sexual characteristics appear (voice change, body hair, breast development).
  • Importance of hygiene, contraception, and prevention of sexually transmitted diseases (STDs).
7. Quick Revision Tips
  • Remember differences between asexual and sexual reproduction.
  • Learn male and female reproductive systems in humans.
  • Understand pollination, fertilization, and seed formation in plants.
  • Know examples of asexual reproduction in animals and plants.
  • Revise adolescence changes and importance of reproductive health.
