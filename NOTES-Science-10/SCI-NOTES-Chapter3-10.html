Class 10 Science Notes: Chapter 3 - Metals and Non-Metals

1. Introduction

Metals and non-metals are elements with distinct physical and chemical properties. Metals are typically shiny, malleable, ductile, and good conductors of heat and electricity. Non-metals are dull, brittle (if solid), and poor conductors.

2. Physical Properties of Metals
  • Lustre: shiny appearance
  • Malleable: can be hammered into sheets
  • Ductile: can be drawn into wires
  • Good conductors of heat and electricity
  • High melting and boiling points
  • Mostly solid at room temperature (except mercury)
  • High density
3. Physical Properties of Non-Metals
  • Dull appearance
  • Brittle if solid
  • Poor conductors of heat and electricity (except graphite)
  • Low melting and boiling points
  • Exist in solid, liquid, or gaseous state at room temperature
  • Low density
4. Chemical Properties of Metals
  • React with oxygen to form basic oxides (e.g., 2Mg + O2 → 2MgO)
  • React with water:
    • Reactive metals (Na, K) react with cold water
    • Less reactive metals (Mg) react with hot water
  • React with acids to form salt and hydrogen gas
  • Form cations by losing electrons
5. Chemical Properties of Non-Metals
  • React with oxygen to form acidic oxides (e.g., CO2, SO2)
  • Generally do not react with water
  • React with metals to form ionic compounds
  • Form covalent compounds with other non-metals
6. Reactivity Series of Metals

Metals are arranged in decreasing order of reactivity:

  • Most reactive: K, Na, Ca, Mg
  • Least reactive: Au, Pt
Example: Zn + CuSO4 → ZnSO4 + Cu
7. Corrosion and Rusting
  • Corrosion: gradual destruction of metals by chemical reaction with air, water, or chemicals.
  • Rusting: corrosion of iron in presence of moisture and air.
  • Prevention:
    • Galvanization (coating with zinc)
    • Painting, oiling, or greasing
    • Alloy formation (stainless steel)
8. Important Compounds of Metals and Non-Metals
  • Metal oxides: basic oxides (Na2O, CaO), amphoteric oxides (Al2O3)
  • Non-metal oxides: acidic oxides (CO2, SO2)
  • Salts: ionic compounds formed by neutralization of acid and base (NaCl, CaSO4)
9. Uses of Metals and Non-Metals
  • Metals: construction (Fe, Al), electrical wiring (Cu, Al), coins (Cu, Ni)
  • Non-metals: fertilizers (N, P), disinfectants (Cl), lubricants (S), medicines (Iodine)
10. Quick Revision Tips
  • Remember physical and chemical differences between metals and non-metals
  • Practice reactions with water, oxygen, and acids
  • Learn reactivity series for displacement reactions
  • Understand corrosion and rusting and prevention methods
