Exercise 10.1 – Tangent to a Circle

Q1. Tangent at any point of a circle is perpendicular to radius.

If circle has center O, and tangent touches at point P
Then OP ⟂ tangent at P
This property is used to construct tangents.

Q2. Draw tangents from a point outside the circle

Steps:
1. Draw circle with center O
2. Mark external point P
3. Join OP, find midpoint M of OP
4. Draw circle with center M, radius MP
5. Intersect original circle at A and B
6. Tangents PA and PB are required tangents

Exercise 10.2 – Properties of Tangents

Q1. Length of tangents from an external point

If external point P at distance d from center O, radius r
Length of tangent = √(d² – r²)

Q2. Two tangents from external point are equal

Tangents from same external point P to a circle are equal in length
So PA = PB
ΔOPA and ΔOPB are congruent (RHS criterion)

Exercise 10.3 – Angle Properties

Q1. Angle between radius and chord

Angle subtended by chord at center = 2 × angle subtended at circle
Angle between tangent and chord = angle in alternate segment

Q2. Cyclic quadrilateral properties

Sum of opposite angles = 180°
Used to solve problems involving inscribed angles

Exercise 10.4 – Problems on Tangents

Q1. Tangents drawn from external point 13 cm from center of circle radius 5 cm

Length of tangent = √(d² – r²) = √(13² – 5²) = √(169 – 25) = √144 = 12 cm

Q2. Two tangents drawn from external point; find angle between them

Angle between tangents = 2 × angle between line joining center and external point with tangents
Use triangle formed with center and tangents

Summary – Circles

  • Tangent to a circle is perpendicular to radius at point of contact
  • Two tangents from an external point are equal
  • Length of tangent = √(distance² – radius²)
  • Angle in alternate segment theorem
  • Cyclic quadrilateral: sum of opposite angles = 180°
  • Properties used to solve geometry problems involving circles and tangents
