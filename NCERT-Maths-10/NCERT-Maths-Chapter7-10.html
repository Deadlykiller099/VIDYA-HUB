Exercise 7.1 – Distance Formula

Q1. Find distance between points A(3, 4) and B(7, 1).

Distance formula:
AB = √[(x₂ – x₁)² + (y₂ – y₁)²]
AB = √[(7 – 3)² + (1 – 4)²]
   = √[16 + 9] = √25 = 5

Q2. Distance between P(–2, 5) and Q(3, –1)

PQ = √[(3 + 2)² + (–1 – 5)²]
   = √[25 + 36] = √61

Exercise 7.2 – Section Formula

Q1. Find coordinates of point dividing AB(2, 3) and B(8, 7) internally in ratio 1:3

Section formula:
x = (mx₂ + nx₁)/(m+n), y = (my₂ + ny₁)/(m+n)
Here, m:n = 1:3
x = (1*8 + 3*2)/4 = (8 + 6)/4 = 14/4 = 7/2
y = (1*7 + 3*3)/4 = (7 + 9)/4 = 16/4 = 4
Point = (7/2, 4)

Q2. Midpoint of PQ(–3, 5) and R(7, –1)

Midpoint formula: (x₁ + x₂)/2 , (y₁ + y₂)/2
= (–3 + 7)/2 , (5 –1)/2 = (4/2 , 4/2) = (2, 2)

Exercise 7.3 – Area of Triangle

Q1. Find area of ΔABC with vertices A(1, 2), B(3, 5), C(5, 2)

Area formula:
Area = 1/2 |x₁(y₂–y₃) + x₂(y₃–y₁) + x₃(y₁–y₂)|
= 1/2 |1(5–2) + 3(2–2) + 5(2–5)|
= 1/2 |3 + 0 –15| = 1/2 |–12| = 6

Q2. ΔPQR with P(0,0), Q(4,0), R(0,3)

Area = 1/2 |0(0–3) + 4(3–0) + 0(0–0)| 
      = 1/2 |0 + 12 + 0| = 6

Exercise 7.4 – Collinearity of Points

Q1. Check if A(1,2), B(3,6), C(5,10) are collinear

Three points are collinear if area of triangle = 0

Area = 1/2 |1(6–10) + 3(10–2) + 5(2–6)|
= 1/2 |–4 + 24 –20| = 1/2 |0| = 0 → Collinear

Q2. Are P(0,0), Q(2,3), R(3,5) collinear?

Area = 1/2 |0(3–5) + 2(5–0) + 3(0–3)| 
= 1/2 |0 +10 –9| = 1/2 |1| = 0.5 ≠ 0 → Not collinear

Summary – Coordinate Geometry

  • Distance formula: √[(x₂ – x₁)² + (y₂ – y₁)²]
  • Section formula (internal division): (mx₂ + nx₁)/(m+n), (my₂ + ny₁)/(m+n)
  • Midpoint formula: ((x₁ + x₂)/2 , (y₁ + y₂)/2)
  • Area of triangle: 1/2 |x₁(y₂–y₃) + x₂(y₃–y₁) + x₃(y₁–y₂)|
  • Three points are collinear if area = 0
