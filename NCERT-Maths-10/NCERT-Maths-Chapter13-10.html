Exercise 14.1 – Mean of Grouped Data

Q1. Find the mean of data: 2, 3, 4, 5, 6

Mean = (Sum of observations) / (Number of observations)
= (2 + 3 + 4 + 5 + 6) / 5
= 20 / 5 = 4

Q2. Find mean for grouped data

Class   Frequency (f)   Midpoint (x)
0-10       5             5
10-20      7             15
20-30      8             25
30-40      10            35

Mean = Σ(f × x) / Σf
= (5×5 + 7×15 + 8×25 + 10×35) / (5+7+8+10)
= (25 + 105 + 200 + 350) / 30 = 680 / 30 ≈ 22.67

Exercise 14.2 – Median of Grouped Data

Q1. Find median of given frequency table

Class       Frequency (f)   Cumulative Frequency (CF)
0-10           5               5
10-20          7               12
20-30          8               20
30-40          10              30

Median class = class where CF ≥ N/2 = 30/2 = 15 → 20-30
Median = l + [(N/2 – CF_prev)/f] × h
= 20 + [(15 – 12)/8] × 10
= 20 + (3/8) × 10 = 20 + 3.75 = 23.75

Exercise 14.3 – Mode of Grouped Data

Q1. Find mode from frequency table

Class   f
0-10     5
10-20    7
20-30    12
30-40    10

Modal class = 20-30 (largest frequency)
Mode = l + [(f1 – f0)/(2f1 – f0 – f2)] × h
f1 = 12, f0 = 7, f2 = 10, l = 20, h = 10
Mode = 20 + [(12–7)/(24–7–10)]×10
= 20 + (5/7)×10 ≈ 27.14

Exercise 14.4 – Graphical Representation

Q1. Represent data by histogram

Steps:
1. Draw axes: x → classes, y → frequency
2. Draw rectangles of width equal to class intervals
3. Height = frequency
4. Adjacent rectangles touch each other

Q2. Represent data by cumulative frequency curve (ogive)

Steps:
1. Draw axes: x → upper class boundary, y → cumulative frequency
2. Plot points for cumulative frequency at upper boundary
3. Join points smoothly
4. Curve represents ogive

Summary – Statistics

  • Mean = Σ(f × x) / Σf
  • Median = l + [(N/2 – CF_prev)/f] × h
  • Mode = l + [(f1 – f0)/(2f1 – f0 – f2)] × h
  • Histogram: frequency vs class intervals
  • Ogive: cumulative frequency curve
