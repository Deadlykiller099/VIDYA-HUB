Exercise 6.1 – Similarity of Triangles

Q1. Show that two triangles are similar by AA criterion.

If two angles of a triangle are equal to two angles of another triangle,
then the triangles are similar (AA similarity).

Example:
ΔABC and ΔPQR, ∠A = ∠P, ∠B = ∠Q → ΔABC ~ ΔPQR

Q2. Sides of similar triangles are proportional.

If ΔABC ~ ΔPQR,
then AB/PQ = BC/QR = AC/PR
This property is often used to find missing sides.

Exercise 6.2 – Proof of Pythagoras Theorem using Similarity

Q1. Proof using perpendicular from hypotenuse vertex:

ΔABC is right angled at B
Draw BD perpendicular to AC
Then ΔABC ~ ΔADB ~ ΔBDC (AA similarity)
Use ratios of sides:
AB² = AD × AC
BC² = DC × AC
Add: AB² + BC² = (AD + DC) × AC = AC²

Exercise 6.3 – Areas of Similar Triangles

Q1. Ratio of areas of similar triangles:

If ΔABC ~ ΔPQR,
Then area(ΔABC)/area(ΔPQR) = (AB/PQ)²
Square of corresponding sides ratio.

Q2. Example:

ΔABC ~ ΔPQR
AB = 4 cm, PQ = 6 cm
Area of ΔABC = 24 cm²
Find area of ΔPQR

(area ratio) = (4/6)² = 16/36 = 4/9
Let area of ΔPQR = x
24/x = 4/9 → x = 54 cm²

Exercise 6.4 – Application of Similarity

Q1. Find height of a tree using shadow:

Let shadow of tree = 15 m, shadow of pole = 3 m, height of pole = 2 m
ΔTree & ΔPole similar
Height of tree / 15 = 2 / 3 → Height = 10 m

Q2. Bridge problem:

Distance of point from two poles, use similar triangles,
Apply ratio of heights and corresponding lengths

Exercise 6.5 – Pythagoras Theorem Applications

Q1. Ladder problem:

Ladder touches wall at height = ?
Distance from wall = ?
Use Pythagoras theorem:
Height² + Base² = Ladder²
Solve for required value

Q2. Distance between two points diagonally across square/rectangle:

Use Pythagoras:
Diagonal² = Length² + Breadth² → Diagonal = √(L² + B²)

Summary – Triangles

  • AA criterion: Two triangles are similar if two angles equal
  • SSS/ SAS similarity also exists
  • Ratios of corresponding sides of similar triangles are equal
  • Areas ratio = (corresponding sides ratio)²
  • Pythagoras theorem: a² + b² = c² in right triangles
  • Applications include heights, distances, shadows, ladder problems
