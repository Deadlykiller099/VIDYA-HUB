Exercise 8.1 – Trigonometric Ratios

Q1. Define trigonometric ratios for angle θ in right triangle ABC

sin θ = Opposite / Hypotenuse = BC / AC
cos θ = Adjacent / Hypotenuse = AB / AC
tan θ = Opposite / Adjacent = BC / AB
cot θ = Adjacent / Opposite = AB / BC
sec θ = Hypotenuse / Adjacent = AC / AB
cosec θ = Hypotenuse / Opposite = AC / BC

Q2. Given right triangle with BC = 3, AB = 4, AC = 5. Find sin A, cos A, tan A

sin A = BC / AC = 3 / 5
cos A = AB / AC = 4 / 5
tan A = BC / AB = 3 / 4
cot A = 4 / 3
sec A = 5 / 4
cosec A = 5 / 3

Exercise 8.2 – Trigonometric Identities

Q1. Prove sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

In right triangle:
sin θ = opposite / hypotenuse = BC / AC
cos θ = adjacent / hypotenuse = AB / AC

sin²θ + cos²θ = (BC² + AB²) / AC² = AC² / AC² = 1

Q2. Find other ratios if sin θ = 3/5

sin θ = 3/5 → opposite = 3, hypotenuse = 5
Adjacent = √(5² – 3²) = √16 = 4
cos θ = 4/5
tan θ = 3/4
cot θ = 4/3
sec θ = 5/4
cosec θ = 5/3

Exercise 8.3 – Trigonometric Ratios of Specific Angles

Q1. Find sin, cos, tan of 30°, 45°, 60°

sin 30° = 1/2, cos 30° = √3/2, tan 30° = 1/√3
sin 45° = √2/2, cos 45° = √2/2, tan 45° = 1
sin 60° = √3/2, cos 60° = 1/2, tan 60° = √3

Q2. Verify sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 for θ = 30°

sin²30° + cos²30° = (1/2)² + (√3/2)² = 1/4 + 3/4 = 1 ✅

Exercise 8.4 – Application of Trigonometry

Q1. Height of a tower using angle of elevation

Angle of elevation θ = 30°, distance from tower = 50 m
Height h = distance × tan θ
h = 50 × tan 30° = 50 × 1/√3 ≈ 28.87 m

Q2. Length of shadow of a pole 6 m high, angle of elevation = 45°

tan θ = height / shadow
tan 45° = 6 / shadow → 1 = 6 / shadow → shadow = 6 m

Q3. Ladder problem

Ladder touches wall at angle θ with ground
Height = Ladder × sin θ
Distance from wall = Ladder × cos θ

Summary – Introduction to Trigonometry

  • Six trigonometric ratios: sin, cos, tan, cosec, sec, cot
  • sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
  • tan θ = sin θ / cos θ, cot θ = cos θ / sin θ
  • Right triangle applications: height, distance, shadow, ladder problems
  • Standard angles: 30°, 45°, 60°
