Polynomials — Chapter 2 (Complete Notes & Solutions)

Theory • Examples • Solved Questions

1. Introduction & Basic Definitions

A polynomial in x is an expression of the form:
anxⁿ + an−1xⁿ⁻¹ + ... + a₁x + a₀

  • Degree: highest power of x.
  • Constant polynomial: degree 0.
  • Zero polynomial: all coefficients 0.
  • Zero of a polynomial: a value α such that P(α)=0.

2. Remainder Theorem

If P(x) is divided by (x – a), the remainder is simply P(a).

Example

P(x) = x³ – 2x² + 3x – 5
Remainder when dividing by (x – 2):
P(2)=8 – 8 + 6 – 5 = 1 → remainder = 1

3. Factor Theorem

(x – a) is a factor of P(x) if P(a)=0.

Example

P(x)=x³ + x² – x – 1
Check x=1:
P(1)=1+1–1–1 = 0
→ (x – 1) is a factor.

4. Division Algorithm

P(x) = D(x)Q(x) + R(x)

Where deg(R) < deg(D).

Example

Divide P(x)=x³ – 3x² + 5x – 3 by (x – 2):

P(2)=3 → remainder
Quotient = x² – x + 3

5. Zeros of a Quadratic Polynomial

If P(x)=ax²+bx+c has zeros α and β:

  • α + β = −b/a
  • αβ = c/a

Example

For 2x² – 5x + 3:
α + β = 5/2
αβ = 3/2

6. Factorisation of Polynomials

Example

Factorise x³ – 6x² + 11x – 6

Try x=1 → P(1)=0
Divide by (x – 1) → x² – 5x + 6
Factor again → (x – 2)(x – 3)

Final answer:
(x – 1)(x – 2)(x – 3)

7. Important Algebraic Identities

  • (a ± b)² = a² ± 2ab + b²
  • (a ± b)³ = a³ ± 3a²b + 3ab² ± b³
  • a³ ± b³ = (a ± b)(a² ∓ ab + b²)

8. Solved Examples

Example 1

Find k if (x – 3) is a factor of x³ – 3x² + kx + 6.

P(3) = 27 – 27 + 3k + 6 = 3k + 6
Set = 0 → k = –2.

Example 2

Find a quadratic whose zeros are 2 and –3.

(x – 2)(x + 3) = x² + x – 6

Example 3

Compute α² + β² for polynomial x² – 4x + 3.

α+β = 4
αβ = 3
α²+β² = (α+β)² – 2αβ
       = 16 – 6 = 10

9. Summary

  • Remainder = P(a)
  • (x – a) is a factor ↔ P(a)=0
  • Quadratic: sum & product of zeros = −b/a, c/a
  • Factorisation often starts by testing ±1, ±2, …
