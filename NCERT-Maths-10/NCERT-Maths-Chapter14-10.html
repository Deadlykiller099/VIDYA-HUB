Probability

Exercise 15.1 – Introduction to Probability

Q1. Probability of getting head in a coin toss

Sample space S = {H, T}, favorable outcomes = {H}
P(H) = Number of favorable / Total outcomes = 1/2

Q2. Probability of getting 3 on a die

Sample space S = {1,2,3,4,5,6}, favorable = {3}
P(3) = 1/6

Exercise 15.2 – Probability of Events

Q1. Probability of rolling an even number on a die

Even numbers = {2,4,6}, total outcomes = 6
P(even) = 3/6 = 1/2

Q2. Probability of drawing a red card from a deck

Total cards = 52, red cards = 26
P(red) = 26/52 = 1/2

Exercise 15.3 – Complementary Events & Word Problems

Q1. Probability of not getting a 5 on a die

P(not 5) = 1 – P(5) = 1 – 1/6 = 5/6

Q2. Probability of getting a number less than 5 on a die

Numbers less than 5 = {1,2,3,4} → 4 favorable outcomes
Total outcomes = 6
P(number < 5) = 4/6 = 2/3

Q3. Probability of drawing an ace from a deck

Total aces = 4, total cards = 52
P(ace) = 4/52 = 1/13
P(not ace) = 1 – 1/13 = 12/13

Summary – Probability

  • Probability of event E: P(E) = Number of favorable outcomes / Total outcomes
  • 0 ≤ P(E) ≤ 1
  • Complementary events: P(not E) = 1 – P(E)
  • Sample space: All possible outcomes
  • Used for coins, dice, cards, and real-life events
