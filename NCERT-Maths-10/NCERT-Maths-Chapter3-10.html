Pair of Linear Equations in Two Variables

Exercise 3.1

Q1. Check whether the pair of equations is consistent:

(a) 2x + 3y = 7
    2x – 4y = –2

a₁/a₂ = 2/2 = 1
b₁/b₂ = 3/–4 = –¾
Since a₁/a₂ ≠ b₁/b₂ → one solution → consistent.

(b) 3x – y = 5
    6x – 2y = 10

a₁/a₂ = 3/6 = 1/2
b₁/b₂ = –1/–2 = 1/2
c₁/c₂ = 5/10 = 1/2
All equal → ∞ solutions → consistent & dependent.

Q2. Form the pair of linear equations for the given problems.

(a) Ratio of incomes of A and B is 3:5, income of A increases by ₹1000 & B decreases by ₹1000 → ratio becomes 4:5.

Let A = 3k, B = 5k
(3k + 1000)/(5k – 1000) = 4/5
Cross-multiply → 15k + 5000 = 20k – 4000
→ 9k = 9000 → k = 1000
Equations:
A – 3k = 0
B – 5k = 0

Exercise 3.2 – Solving by Graphical Method

Q1. Draw graphs and find the solution:

(a) x + y = 4
    x – y = 2

Solve algebraically:
Add → 2x = 6 → x = 3
Then y = 1
Solution: (3, 1)

Q2. Whether the lines intersect, parallel or coincident?

1) 2x + 3y = 6
   4x + 6y = 12

Ratios equal → coincident → ∞ solutions.

2) 2x – 3y = 8
   4x – 6y = 9

a₁/a₂ = b₁/b₂ but ≠ c₁/c₂ → parallel → no solution.

Exercise 3.3 – Algebraic Methods (Substitution)

Q1. Solve using substitution:

(a) x + y = 14
    x – y = 4

Add → 2x = 18 → x = 9
Then y = 5
Solution: (9, 5)

(b) 2x + 3y = 11
    2x – 4y = –24

Subtract → 7y = 35 → y = 5
Then 2x + 15 = 11 → 2x = –4 → x = –2
Solution: (–2, 5)

Exercise 3.3 – Algebraic Methods (Elimination)

Q2. Solve using elimination method:

(a) 3x + 4y = 10
    2x – 2y = 2

Multiply second eq. by 2:
4x – 4y = 4

Add:
7x = 14 → x = 2
Then 3(2)+4y=10 → y=1
Solution: (2, 1)

(b) 5x – 6y = –2
    3x + 2y = 14

Multiply (2nd eq) by 3:
9x + 6y = 42

Add with first:
14x = 40 → x = 40/14 = 20/7
Substitute to get y = 11/7
Solution: (20/7, 11/7)

Exercise 3.4 – Cross Multiplication Method

Q1. Solve:

(a) 3x – y – 3 = 0
    9x – 3y – 9 = 0

Ratios a₁/a₂ = b₁/b₂ = c₁/c₂ → ∞ solutions.

(b) x + 2y = 5
    3x – y = 5

Cross-multiplication:
x/ (5(-1) – (5*2)) = y/ (1*5 – 3*5) = 1/ (1*(-1) – (3*2))

Solve:
x/(-15) = y/(-10) = 1/(-7)

x = (15/7), y = (10/7)
Solution: (15/7, 10/7)

Summary – Chapter 3

  • Consistent → one or ∞ solutions
  • Inconsistent → no solution
  • Methods: Graphical, Substitution, Elimination, Cross-Multiplication
  • a₁/a₂ = b₁/b₂ = c₁/c₂ → ∞ solutions
  • a₁/a₂ = b₁/b₂ ≠ c₁/c₂ → no solution
  • Otherwise → one unique solution
