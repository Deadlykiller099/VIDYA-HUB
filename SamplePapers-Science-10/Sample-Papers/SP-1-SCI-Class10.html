Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (1 × 20 = 20)

Q1.

Which gas is released during photosynthesis? (a) Oxygen (b) Carbon dioxide (c) Nitrogen (d) Hydrogen

Q2.

Ohm's law is valid for: (a) All conductors (b) Metallic conductors (c) Semiconductors (d) All non-metals

Q3.

The pH of neutral water is: (a) 0 (b) 7 (c) 14 (d) 1

Q4.

Which of these is not a part of the human brain? (a) Cerebrum (b) Cerebellum (c) Medulla (d) Retina

Q5.

The main function of red blood cells is: (a) Clotting (b) Carry oxygen (c) Fight infection (d) Transport hormones

Q6.

Which of these is a non-renewable source of energy? (a) Solar (b) Wind (c) Coal (d) Hydropower

Q7.

Which part of the eye controls the amount of light entering? (a) Retina (b) Cornea (c) Iris (d) Lens

Q8.

Which of the following is a greenhouse gas? (a) Oxygen (b) Methane (c) Helium (d) Nitrogen

Q9.

Which blood component helps in clotting? (a) RBC (b) WBC (c) Platelets (d) Plasma

Q10.

Ohm's law formula is: (a) V=IR (b) P=VI (c) V=I/P (d) R=VI

Q11.

Which of the following is a macronutrient? (a) Iron (b) Nitrogen (c) Zinc (d) Copper

Q12.

The blue color of the sky is due to: (a) Reflection (b) Scattering (c) Diffraction (d) Refraction

Q13.

Which gas is used in respiration? (a) Oxygen (b) Nitrogen (c) Carbon dioxide (d) Hydrogen

Q14.

The process of breakdown of glucose in absence of oxygen is: (a) Aerobic respiration (b) Anaerobic respiration (c) Photosynthesis (d) Transpiration

Q15.

Which part of the neuron receives signals? (a) Axon (b) Dendrites (c) Soma (d) Myelin

Q16.

Which of these is a vector quantity? (a) Speed (b) Velocity (c) Distance (d) Time

Q17.

Which process in plants releases water vapor? (a) Photosynthesis (b) Transpiration (c) Respiration (d) Evaporation

Q18.

Which device converts chemical energy into electrical energy? (a) Motor (b) Cell (c) Generator (d) Transformer

Q19.

Which type of reproduction produces genetically identical offspring? (a) Sexual (b) Asexual (c) Both (d) None

Q20.

The organ responsible for filtering blood in humans is: (a) Heart (b) Kidney (c) Liver (d) Lungs
